Award-Winning

Studio Ghibli has produced 15 of Japan’s highest-grossing anime films. While My Neighbor Totoro is its most recognizable, the studio’s first major hit was Kiki’s Delivery Service (1989). Porco Rosso (1992) also topped the Japanese box office, and Spirited Away (2003) became the first non-English-language film to win the Oscar for Best Animated Feature, as well as Japan’s highest-grossing film at the time.

Captivating Themes and Storytelling

Studio Ghibli's common themes are about the coming of age, personal growth, having a deep respect for nature and the romanticization of everyday simple life. Love is also common, but unlike fairy-tale romance, love is about maturity across friendships, family, and self-discovery. Many films feature strong female protagonists who are active saviors of their own stories. Whisper of the Heart exemplifies this, following Suzuki, a young girl discovering her purpose, with a strong supportive relationship with Seiji.

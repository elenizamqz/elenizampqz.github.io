The Founders of Ghibli

In 1985 Studio Ghibli was founded by Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata, and Toshio Suzuki. Over 37 years, they’ve produced 20 feature films, along with shorts and commercials.

The Beginnings of Studio Ghibli

Takahata and Miyazaki met in the 1960s at Tôei Dôga and worked together for over a decade. In 1985, with funding from Tokuma Shoten, they and producer Toshio Suzuki founded Studio Ghibli near Tokyo. Their first feature, Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind, was a hit, followed by Laputa: Castle in the Sky in 1986. Unlike other studios, Ghibli focused on heartfelt, poetic storytelling, exploring human experience.

Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki was born on January 5, 1941, to Katsuji and Dola Miyazaki. His father’s work on Zero Fighter planes inspired his lifelong love of aircraft, while his mother’s battle with spinal tuberculosis influenced his strong, resilient female characters, such as in My Neighbor Totoro. Miyazaki graduated from Gakushūin University in 1963 with a degree in economics and joined Tōei Animation, where he met lifelong collaborator Isao Takahata and his future wife, Akemi Ōta. After leaving Tōei in 1971, he worked on projects including Panda! Go Panda! and his first full-length film, Lupin III: Castle of Cagliostro (1979).

Isao Takahata

Isao Takahata was born in Ujiyamada (now Ise) in 1935, the youngest of seven children. At age nine, he survived a US bombing of his hometown. He graduated from the University of Tokyo in French literature and was inspired by animator Paul Grimault’s Le Roi et l’oiseau to pursue animation. Takahata began his career at Toei Doga, where he met Hayao Miyazaki, forming a lifelong friendship and collaboration.

Toshio Suzuki

Toshio Suzuki, born August 19, 1948, in Nagoya, Japan, studied literature at Keio University, fostering a love for storytelling that shaped his animation career. He became an editor at Animage, where he supported Hayao Miyazaki’s Nausicaä manga—key to founding Studio Ghibli. As Ghibli’s producer, Suzuki balanced creative freedom with practical guidance, helping create classics like My Neighbor Totoro and Kiki’s Delivery Service. He also mentored new talent, ensuring the studio’s legacy.

Why is it called Studio Ghibli?

“Ghibli” comes from the Italian word for the hot Sahara wind, symbolizing the studio’s goal to bring fresh ideas to anime. The name also reflects Miyazaki’s love of Italy and aviation, inspired by the Caproni Ca.309 Ghibli, a WWII Italian aircraft.
